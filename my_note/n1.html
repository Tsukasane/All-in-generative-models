Preliminaries

For a generative model

  • We want to sample a latent z from a Gaussian distribution z～N(μ,σ) (maybe is not a standard normal distribution).

  • Due to the uncertainty of random sampling，z is not directly derivable.

  • Thus, we use the re-parameterization trick to sample z:=N(z;μ,σI). Representated by the format below, the randomness is switched to ϵ, then z becomes derivable.

    z=μ+σ⊙ϵ,ϵ～N(0,1)

    PS: ⊙ is element-wise product.

Mathematical Theorem

Theorem1: if two Gaussian distribution have the same mean value. ϵ1ϵ2→ϵ~

N(0,σ12I)+N(0,σ22I)～N(0,（σ12+σ22）I)

Theorem2：Bayesian Formulas

P(A|B)=P(A)P(B|A)P(B)...(b−0)P(AB)=P(A)P(B|A)...(b−1)P(ABC)=P(A)P(B|A)...(b−2)P(A|B)=P(AB)P(B)...(b−3)

Theorem3: for a Gaussian distribution

x∼N(μ,σ)∝exp⁡(−(x−μ)22σ2)=exp⁡(−12(1σ2x2−2μσ2x+μ2σ2))

 

Diffuse, Reverse diffuse, and Denoise

Diffuse: q(xt|xt−1)

Definition: q:xt～N(1−βtxt−1,βt),0<βt<1

PS: β is a pre-defined parameter.

As the rules of the Markov Chain, the state xt only depends on the transmission metric of its previous state xt−1. The transmission metric depends on the probability distribution q, which is defined by its mean and variance.

xt=q(xt|xt−1)=N(xt;1−βtxt−1,βt⋅I)

Then we have

xt=1−βtxt−1+βt⊙ϵ,ϵ～N(0,1)

As the timestep t gets larger, xt has more weight on noise and less weight on its previous state. The context from x0 'faded', and the noise becomes dominant.

However, for a generative model, what we want to do is generating a real image from latent, which is the reverse process of the forward diffusion stated above.

Reverse-Diffuse: q(xt−1|xt)

When the forward process q(xt|xt−1) is a normal distribution, its reverse process q(xt−1|xt) is also a normal distribution (skip a prove here), but q(xt−1|xt) is intractable.

Thus, for training, we actually intend to find a probability model θ∈Θ，which can map the latent to the real world image as good as possible. The generated image should have a distribution as similar as the real world images (a domain defined by the training dataset).

PS: Here, the reverse q represent the true reverse diffusion process, which is irrelevant to the model parameter θ.

 

Although x0 and xt are not independent variables, we still list them seperately in the condition position, to show their special meanings.

q(xt−1|(xt,x0))=q(xt,x0,xt−1)q(xt,x0)

According to Theorem2, we have

q(x0)q(xt−1|x0)q(xt|(xt−1,x0))q(x0)q(xt|x0)
q(xt|xt−1,x0)⋅q(xt−1|x0)q(xt|x0)⇒q(xt|xt−1)⋅q(xt−1|x0)q(xt|x0)

The numerator and the denominatorlatter of the latter term all depend on information of θ, while the former term is a forward diffusion process. As a strictly defined Markov process, it is not relevant to x0.

Denoise: pθ(xt−1|xt)

pθ(xt−1|xt,x0) indicates denoising from a specific time stamp, and the latent depends on a specific input sample x0

Recall that αt and βt are predefined parameters, βt~ serves as the σ2 in Theorem3 and μ~(xt,x0) serves as the μ in Theorem3

Therefore, we have deduced that xt has a analytical solution under Gaussian distribution.

xt:=N(xt−1;μt~,σt~)=N(xt−1;1αt(xt−1−αt1−αtϵt))

with the condition of T is large enough and β is small enough.

Conclusion：the forward and backward process have consistency in mathematical representation, and both of them have analytical solutions.

 

β is designed to be in the range of (0,0001, 0,02)

In the generating process, the denoising doesn't have to be step by step, for larger step leading to stronger diversity.

For the setup of β, the original DDPM samples it linearly. Intuitively, bigger step is preferred when the noise is heavy. The value is larger when the timestep approximating t and gets smaller when the timestep approximating 0.
